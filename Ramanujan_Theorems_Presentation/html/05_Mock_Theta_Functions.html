Ramanujan’s Mock Theta Functions¶

In the final months of his life (1919–1920), Ramanujan wrote letters to G. H. Hardy describing a new type of function he called "Mock Theta Functions".

He listed 17 examples, but without formal definitions.
These mysterious functions seemed similar to the classical theta functions of modular form theory — yet they did not behave exactly like modular forms.

For decades, mathematicians puzzled over what “mock” meant, until Sander Zwegers (2002) showed that Ramanujan’s mock theta functions are related to harmonic Maass forms — completing a century-old mystery.

A Simple Example¶

One of Ramanujan’s third-order mock theta functions is:

$$ f(q) = 1 + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{q^{n^2}}{(1+q)^2 (1+q^2)^2 \cdots (1+q^n)^2} $$

We can approximate ( f(q) ) numerically for ( |q| < 1 ) and explore how it behaves as ( q \to 1^- ).

def mock_theta_f(q, n_terms=50):
    """
    Compute a truncated approximation of Ramanujan’s third-order mock theta function f(q).
    f(q) = 1 + sum_{n=1}^∞ q^{n^2} / ∏_{k=1}^{n} (1 + q^k)^2
    """
    total = 1.0
    for n in range(1, n_terms + 1):
        numerator = q**(n**2)
        denom = np.prod([(1 + q**k)**2 for k in range(1, n + 1)])
        total += numerator / denom
    return total

q_values = np.linspace(0.1, 0.99, 50)
f_values = np.array([mock_theta_f(q, n_terms=50) for q in q_values])

plt.figure(figsize=(9,6))
plt.plot(q_values, np.real(f_values), label="Re[f(q)]", linewidth=2)
plt.plot(q_values, np.imag(f_values), '--', label="Im[f(q)]", linewidth=2)
plt.title("Ramanujan’s Third-Order Mock Theta Function f(q)")
plt.xlabel("q")
plt.ylabel("f(q)")
plt.grid(True, alpha=0.6)
plt.legend()
plt.show()

Mathematical Significance¶

Ramanujan’s mock theta functions are now understood as holomorphic parts of harmonic Maass forms — functions that generalize modular forms by allowing a non-holomorphic “shadow” term.

In modern notation, a mock theta function ( f(q) ) is paired with a modular form ( g(q) ) such that:

$$ \widehat{f}(q) = f(q) + g^*(q) $$

where ( g^*(q) ) is a non-holomorphic integral transform of ( g(q) ).

These discoveries revealed deep connections between:

  • Number theory,
  • Modular forms,
  • String theory, and
  • Black hole entropy (via modern physics interpretations of modularity).

Ramanujan anticipated — without formal proof — structures that wouldn’t be fully understood until nearly a century later.
This is perhaps the clearest example of his mathematical intuition operating far ahead of its time.

Discussion¶

Ramanujan’s Mock Theta Functions represent the culmination of his mathematical journey.

  • They bridge the gap between classical modular forms and modern number theory.
  • Their structure inspired major breakthroughs in the Langlands program, string theory, and quantum modular forms.
  • The rediscovery of their meaning by Zwegers (2002) and subsequent work by Ono and Bringmann confirmed Ramanujan’s deep foresight.
